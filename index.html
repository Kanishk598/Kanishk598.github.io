I am a roboticist

I am an undergraduate student at BITS Pilani majoring in Mechanical Engineering. I am a robotics and computer vision engineer who loves electronics and antennaes. I am leading the Navigation subsystem at Sally Robotics, researching on robotics at CEERI, and making a Mars rover with CRISS.

Previously I worked with Intelligent Mobility Labs on robotic state estimation, autonomous navigation, and integrated the code of various teams. I am also the outreach and executive committee member of The Radio Astronomy Club, a part of Computer Science Association, and the Joint Coordinator of Mechanical Engineering Association.

All play and no work

I have worked on various projects spanning robotics, machine learning, web development and algorithm designing. Below are some of my key contributions.

State Estimation

I have worked on various projects on state estimation and localization. Two of my prominent SLAM implementations are LSD-SLAM and ORB-SLAM3 in which I used the TUM's AR-Drone in ROS Kinetic. Currently I am building an end-to-end SLAM pipeline for Sally's autonomous car.



Autonomous Navigation

This is an ongoing project of mine in which I developed the ROS navigation stack with a dynamic waypoint follower. I am building custom path planners on top of this navigation stack that are to be used on the Mars rover being developed by CRISS Robotics.

Computer Vision

I predominantly work in computer vision involving feature detection & description, feature matching, 3D registration, visual odometry and deep learning. I worked on FAST algorithm, Object Recognition, and currently working on RL-CV based autonomous driving control.



Robot Controller

I made a robot controller in JavaScript and linked it to the TUM's AR-Drone running on another computer. ROSLibJS and python websocket were used in connecting the two devices over WiFi and transferring the control commands from one device to another.
