I am a roboticist.

I am an undergraduate student at BITS Pilani majoring in Mechanical Engineering. I am a robotics and computer vision engineer who loves electronics and antennaes. I am leading the Navigation subsystem at Sally Robotics, developing an augmented reality system at Quidich, making a Mars rover with CRISS, and researching on robotics at CEERI.

Previously I worked with Intelligent Mobility Labs where I worked on robotic state estimation, autonomous navigation, and integrated the code of various teams. I am also the outreach and executive committee member of The Radio Astronomy Club, a part of Computer Science Association, and the Joint Coordinator of Mechanical Engineering Association.

All play and no work.

I have worked on various projects spanning robotics, machine learning, web development and algorithm designing. Below are some of my key contributions.

State Estimation

I have worked on various projects on state estimation and localization. Two of my prominent SLAM implementations are LSD-SLAM and ORB-SLAM3 in which I used the TUM's AR-Drone in ROS Kinetic.



Autonomous Navigation

This is an ongoing project of mine in which I developed the ROS navigation stack. I am building the Anytime Dynamic A* on top of this navigation stack that is to be used on the Mars rover at the University Rover Challenge 2022.

Computer Vision

I predominantly work in computer vision involving feature detection & description, feature matching, 3D registration, visual odometry and deep learning. I worked on FAST algorithm, Object Detection, and currently integrating SuperGlue feature matcher into the PySLAM compilation of feature detectors and matchers.



Robot Controller

I made a robot controller in JavaScript and linked it to the TUM's AR-Drone running on another computer. ROSLibJS and python websocket were used in connecting the two devices over WiFi and transferring the control commands from one device to another.
